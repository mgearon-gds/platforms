GOV.UK tools for everyone

Large or small we have options to support you deliver digital experiences for your users.

  • Pay can take online payments from your users
  • Notify can send emails, text messages and letters to your users
  • Forms can create online form in minutes without needing technical knowledge
  • Sign in let users sign in to your service

What we offer

Our suite of tools includes Pay, Notify, Forms and Sign in. All of the basics to start managing and running online systems

Over 1,400 organisations use Notify and Pay has 769 live services. Notify has sent over 6.2 billion notifications since 2016 and Pay has processed over 41.9 million transactions, handling 2.7 billion pounds.

Supporting you

All of our services offer 9:30am to 5:30pm office hours support. With response time being up to 2 working days.

For those urgent moments of support we also offer out of office hours support.

Low cost and no procurement

All of our tools are free to use to start with. As you receive more payments with Pay or send more notifications with Notify you will then be charged as you go along.

Procurement can be difficult but with all of our services there are no procurement processes.

Our services

Accessibility

All of our services are built with accessibility in mind. We thoroughly test our services to make sure everyone can use the service and constantly look for ways to improve.

As all of the services are built within the civil service they all go through the same Service Standard and quality checks any other Government service would go through

Security and privacy

Our services are designed and secured with the highest standards.

  • Pay provides a Payment Card Industry (PCI) fully compliant payment handling service
  • Notify data is encrypted and complies with data protection law. It goes further with compliance with National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) Cloud Security Principles and protective monitoring to record activity and more
  • Using Forms all completed forms are encrypted and sent securely to an email address you specify
  • Sign in uses the OpenID connect (OIDC) authentication protocol and enforcing two-factor authentication when users sign in to your service

Building bespoke

Pay, Notify and in the future Sign In all have API's you can use to connect your services to. There are no extra costs to using the API's and all of the services have complete documentation on how to start:

  • GOV.UK Pay API documentation
  • GOV.UK Notify API documentation
